Lasagna

a delicious, golden-brown lasagna with layers of pasta, rich meat sauce, and melted cheese on top.

Lasagna originated in Italy, specifically from the region of Emilia-Romagna. It is a traditional Italian dish made by layering pasta sheets with meat sauce, cheese, and béchamel sauce, then baking until golden and bubbly.

This lasagna boasts a savory, rich flavor with a perfect balance of tangy tomato sauce, seasoned meat, creamy cheese, and tender pasta. Each bite offers a comforting blend of textures and a satisfying, hearty taste.

Ingredients:

  • 12 lasagna sheets
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef over medium heat until browned. Add the minced garlic, dried basil, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Cook for another 2-3 minutes. Stir in the marinara sauce and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. In a bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, egg, and half of the grated Parmesan cheese. Mix well.
  4. Cook the lasagna sheets according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  5. In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of the meat sauce on the bottom. Place a layer of lasagna sheets over the sauce. Spread a layer of the ricotta mixture over the pasta, followed by a layer of meat sauce and a sprinkle of shredded mozzarella cheese. Repeat these layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of meat sauce and mozzarella cheese on top. Sprinkle the remaining Parmesan cheese over the top.
  6. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.
  7. Let the lasagna cool for about 10 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh parsley if desired. Enjoy!
