Slow Cooker Baby Back Ribs

These fall-off-the-bone tender ribs are cooked in a slow cooker and finished on the grill for a smoky flavor. Perfect for a summer barbecue!

Origin of the Dish

Baby back ribs are a classic American barbecue dish, popularized in the Southern United States. The technique of slow-cooking ribs and finishing them on the grill has roots in traditional Southern barbecue, where low and slow cooking methods create tender, flavorful meat.

Ingredients:

  • 3 pounds baby back ribs, trimmed
  • salt and ground black pepper, to taste
  • ½ cup water
  • ½ onion, sliced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 (18 ounce) bottle barbecue sauce

Directions

  1. Season the ribs with salt and pepper. Place the ribs in a slow cooker, and add water, onion, and garlic. Cover, and cook on Low for 6 to 8 hours, or on High for 3 to 4 hours.
  2. Preheat an outdoor grill for medium heat, and lightly oil the grate.
  3. Remove the ribs from the slow cooker, and place them on the preheated grill. Brush with barbecue sauce. Grill until heated through and slightly charred, about 5 minutes per side. Brush with additional barbecue sauce before serving.
